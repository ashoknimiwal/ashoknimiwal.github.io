Contextual Thesaurus

Work in Progress

A domain-aware synonym engine built on custom Word2Vec embeddings. Rather than returning generic dictionary synonyms, it learns how language is actually used within a specific domain — so querying "liquid" in a financial context returns cash, assets, solvent, not fluid, watery, wet.

The Problem: Context Changes Everything

Thesaurus is a general-purpose digital dictionary. Its synonyms reflect average language use, which can misleads meaning of domain specific words. The same word can mean entirely different things depending on where it appears:

Word Generic synonyms Financial synonyms
liquid fluid · watery · wet cash · assets · solvent
yield produce · harvest · give return · dividend · coupon
spread butter · paste · smear bid-ask · margin · credit
volume sound · loud · noise trades · shares · turnover

The problem compounds further with specialist vocabulary. Terms like EBITDA, repo rate, delta-hedging, and NAV appear too infrequently in general corpora for any model to learn meaningful representations, leaving them with near-random vectors, and confidently wrong neighbours. Compounding this further, multi-word expressions such as repo rate, quantitative easing, and net asset value are rarely treated as single semantic units; yet in finance, their meaning is inseparable from their combination. Splitting them into unigrams strips away precisely the context that makes them meaningful, underscoring the need for domain-aware phrase detection before any embedding is learned.

💡 Quick note: About the multi word expressions: the good news is that identifying these multi-word expressions does not have to be a manual headache. You can do it automatically before training even begins, by scanning the corpus for word pairs (or triplets) that co-occur together far more often than you would expect by chance. If repo and rate almost always show up side by side, that is a strong statistical signal that they belong together. Once identified, you simply join them with an underscore, turning repo rate into repo_rate, and feed that pre-processed text into word2vec. The model then learns a single, unified embedding for repo_rate as a whole expression, rather than learning separate and potentially misleading vectors for repo and rate individually.

How It Works

The approach is grounded in a simple linguistic observation: "You shall know a word by the company it keeps" (Firth, 1957). Words that appear in similar contexts carry similar meanings. Training a model directly on domain text lets it learn those context patterns from professional language, not everyday prose.

Explore further: If you'd like to develop a deeper intuition for how Word2Vec works, including its architecture, training process, and how the geometry of the embedding space encodes meaning, I wrote a dedicated post on it: Understanding Word2Vec.

Domain corpus ingestion and preprocessing Financial filings (10-K/10-Q), earnings transcripts, and analyst reports provide raw professional text at scale.

2
Word2Vec training (Skip-gram) A shallow neural network learns 300-dimensional word vectors by predicting surrounding context words. No labels or human annotation are required, as the corpus itself acts as the supervision signal, enabling scalable training and emergent semantic structure.
|
3
Query preprocessing spaCy lemmatisation maps banks / banking / banked -> bank, ensuring queries align with how the training corpus was preprocessed.
|
4
Cosine similarity lookup The top-N nearest neighbours in embedding space are returned as domain-accurate synonyms, ranked by semantic closeness. Sub-millisecond inference is achieved through singleton model loading.
|
5
Interactive visualisation Force-directed graph makes the topology of semantic space tangible, with nodes orbiting closer when semantically similar.
